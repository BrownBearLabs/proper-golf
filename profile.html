Profile

When people ask “How did you get into Golf Course Architecture?” I respond, “Hard work, and a lot of luck.” It is the truth, just simplified. My relentless pursuit of becoming an architect has taken me around the world, working on some great projects during this recent slow period for golf. Most architects I would write to over the years had just let go of employees, or had all of their work on hold. That's when I started to teach myself Chinese and learned how to operate heavy equipment, and everything changed. Somehow I managed to gain 5 years of experience building golf courses when so many said it was virtually impossible.

My unlikely path has taken me from boarding school honors projects, to writing a Tillinghast thesis for an independent study class at Hobart College. In art class I made golf course inspired silkscreens. After graduating, as a way to get construction experience, I spent 6-weeks at heavy equipment operating certification school in rural Virginia, and went to work on my first golf construction job a week later. I started most of my mornings for the next 2 years at 5-am working at a number of courses in the NY-NJ region. Then at 25 years old opportunity knocked, and I got to build a golf course in the Sandhills of Nebraska, for Tom Doak. From there I moved on to building another new course for Doak, this time on an Island in China, and at 27 years old, I haven’t looked back.

Along the way, I have caddied at 6 Top100 courses (all Tillinghast), and helped build and restore 3 Top100 courses. One of my shaping projects won Best Resort Renovation. I even helped on the bid for the Rio Olympic golf course with Tom Doak. In 2012 I still managed to play over 70 golf courses, and saw 63 more in 2013. (If you are reading this, then at some point along the way I have probably even wrote you a letter asking to play your course or for a job!)

My fascination with golf courses never seems to stop, as I have seen hundreds of golf courses, and photographed many of them. I’ve counted my Doak Points, and read virtually all of the books on the subject. I was invited to serve on a panel for The Golf Hole Hall of Fame, and rate courses for a magazine. You can find my pictures in the USGA Architectural Archives, and Golf Architecture, the magazine, and more...

If nothing else I have worked tirelessly in building my career in architecture. There are less than a handful of people my age with the same amount of experience building golf courses, or seen as many courses, and I am extremely proud of that. As a culmination of my travels, study, and work in golf construction and architecture, I have founded Proper Golf to continue building the thoughtful, fun, low impact and artistic golf I love so much.

Jaeger Kovich
