Design

Overview

Smart, and simple golf courses that are tailored to their environment are the only ones succeeding during the current slow period in golf. The big picture goals of the project must be completely understood before a design concept can be developed. Whether it is for an ideal site in the sand, or a constrained urban project, the design will be made to match the budget and long term goals of the course. While it is always preferable to “find” the golf course, and use the natural features to define the routing and strategies, Proper Golf has the ability to create them when the conditions are less than ideal.

Over the past few decades architects have fallen into the habit of handing over millions of dollars in plans to be constructed by an outside party or contractor. Why pay all that money for the plans when it is only the very beginning of the process? Proper Golf believes that the true design is only in its infancy on paper, and that it must evolve in the field. Every golf course and individual hole has countless problems waiting to be solved, and finding the best solutions can only be done by actively participating in the construction process. Proper Golf prides itself on the Design/Build concept, that Jaeger Kovich learned while working for Tom Doak of Renaissance Golf Design.
